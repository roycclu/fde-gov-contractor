GovTrack — Why Government Contracting?

A market sizing, competitive, and GTM brief for Exa's expansion into the $700B+ federal procurement intelligence space.

Use Case

Every year, the US federal government awards over $700 billion in contracts. To compete, government contractors need to identify relevant opportunities, understand who currently holds the contract (the "incumbent"), track agency budget priorities, and build their pipeline months in advance of solicitation release.

Today, that workflow looks like this:

  • Manual keyword searches on SAM.gov
  • Spreadsheet tracking of agency spending on USASpending.gov
  • Google Alerts for agency news
  • $12–18K/year GovWin IQ subscription — if the firm can afford it

GovTrack replaces that fragmented workflow with a single semantic search interface. A BD manager types "HHS cybersecurity" and surfaces live RFPs, incumbent award history, and agency priority signals — instantly, across sources that keyword search misses.

Where it fits in the BD workflow: GovTrack is a daily-use tool. BD teams open it each morning to check for new solicitations, monitor agency news, and build their opportunity pipeline. It complements (but doesn't require) a formal capture management platform like Shipley or Deltek.

User Persona

Primary — Capture Manager / BD Director

  • Firm size: $5M–$50M annual revenue, 20–250 employees
  • NAICS focus: IT, professional services, logistics, engineering, health IT
  • Pain: Doing the job of a 3-person BD team alone. GovWin is too expensive to justify; SAM.gov is too slow and keyword-limited. Misses opportunities because the search surface is too narrow.
  • Budget authority: Recommends tools to VP/CEO; typical SaaS spend tolerance $150–$500/month per seat
  • Daily behavior: Checks SAM.gov + Google + industry newsletters; maintains an opportunity pipeline in Excel or Salesforce

Secondary — SBIR / 8(a) / New Entrant

  • Early-stage firms with a technical capability but no existing GovCon infrastructure
  • Cannot justify $15K/year for GovWin; price-sensitive; will pay $99–$199/month
  • Need help finding the right agencies and set-aside vehicles before they exist as known entities

The key moat: Exa's neural retrieval catches intent, not just keywords. A BD manager searching "Army decision support" may not use the exact phrase in the solicitation. Keyword tools miss it. Exa finds it.

Market Opportunity

  • Total federal procurement: $737B/year (FY2023)
  • Registered small businesses on SAM.gov: ~500,000
  • Active GovCon firms: ~130,000 (received awards in past 3 years)
  • Addressable at $200/mo: 10,000–30,000 firms → $24M–$72M ARR
  • Expansion to agency procurement offices: adds a separate enterprise segment

Most vendors ignore this market because it requires domain knowledge, government data literacy, and a willingness to work with a fragmented customer base that is skeptical of new SaaS. That friction is Exa's entry point — not a barrier.

Why Exa Wins Here

  • Semantic retrieval catches plain-language opportunities that SAM.gov keyword filters miss — especially early-stage market research notices and industry day announcements
  • Multi-source ingestion eliminates the need to check 4+ sites manually — SAM.gov, USASpending, agency news, procurement trade press all in one query
  • Real-time indexing surfaces opportunities before they hit aggregators like GovWin, giving early movers a 24–48 hour head start on teaming conversations
  • No hallucinations — Exa returns citations, not generated summaries. In a compliance-sensitive market, sourced retrieval is a trust and regulatory advantage

Sales Barriers & How to Overcome Them

Budget cycle timing Target the BD director directly — $200/mo is within discretionary budget at most firms; doesn't need procurement approval.
"SAM.gov is free" Frame around time cost, not tool cost. If a BD manager spends 2 hours/day on manual search at $80/hr, that's $40K/year in hidden cost.

Pilot / POC Design

A compelling pilot runs for 30 days with 2–3 BD managers at a target firm:

  • Week 1: Run their current active searches through GovTrack — compare results to SAM.gov output
  • Week 2: Surface 3 RFPs they would not have found with keywords alone
  • Week 3–4: Pull incumbent intel on their top 5 pipeline opportunities
  • Measure: Opportunities found, time saved, incumbent IDs surfaced
  • Close: Show that Exa found relevant opportunities 24–48 hours before GovWin indexed them
